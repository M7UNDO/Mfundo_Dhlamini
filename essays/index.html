Essay 1: UX & UI Analysis

In today's digital world, the quality of a website's User Interface (UI) and User Experience (UX) play a crucial role in the development of digital products, as it directly impacts how users interact and engage with them (Hamidli, 2023: 1-3 ). UI refers to the visual and interactive elements that users engage with, such as buttons, icons, and the layout, while UX encompasses the broader emotional and cognitive experience of using the digital system. These design elements go beyond the functionality and aesthetics, as they operate as signs and signifiers, forming a semiotic system that communicates meaning to the user.

UI and UX do not only guide the user's actions, but they also influence their interpretation, trust and perception. Thus, UI and UX are systems of communication, and interaction design becomes a language users must read and respond to. Well-designed UI can make a digital product more aesthetically pleasing, intuitive and simple to use, while well-designed UX can ensure a more positive experience throughout the user's journey on the website. Successful implementation of UI and UX design can directly increase user satisfaction, loyalty, and engagement with the digital product (Hamidli, 2023: 1-3 ).

Ghanchi notes that the design of a system carries an ethical weight. This means that any product that is created embodies a value system, clearly indicating the designer's beliefs and moral principles. Poor or manipulative design can exclude, confuse, or even exploit users, while good, ethical design can empower them by being inclusive, accessible and transparent with users (Ghanchi: 2021). This is especially vital when the digital platform serves a vulnerable user base, such as the National Student Financial Aid Scheme (NSFAS). NSFAS was created to offer financial assistance to disadvantaged South African students for their tertiary education.

Essay 2: The internet, geopolitics, and digital coloniality

In the digital age, internet access is no longer a luxury but a necessity for full participation within a society, enabling coordination, the exchange of information and even survival (Kasapis, 2021). The internet is not only a communication medium but also a geopolitical tool, and beneath the assurances of universal access and decentralised networks exists an infrastructure that is increasingly controlled by private entities, whose actions extend much beyond just business interests. This has never been more evident than in the recent geopolitical conflict between Russia and Ukraine, where US-based company SpaceX, headed by Elon Musk played a significant role in shaping the course of war and diplomacy through the deployment of Starlink, the largest satellite mega-constellation globally, delivering Internet service to remote regions.

While initially Starlink received praise and functioned as lifeline for Ukraine offering critical infrastructure in a crisis, the involvement of a private corporation in warfare have raised concerns about digital coloniality, where colonial power structures are being replicated by the technological dominance of Western entities.

This essay critically examines the intersections between internet governance, geopolitical power, and digital coloniality through the case study of SpaceX's Starlink satellite internet service in Ukraine between 2022 and 2024. By drawing on theories of digital geography as well as political economy this essay highlights how Starlink's intervention represents a paradigm shift in the way that digital infrastructure serving as both a tool of resistance and a means of neo-colonial domination. The analysis identifies key aspects of transformation which include: the privatisation of essential communication infrastructures in areas of conflict, the rise of corporate entities as geopolitical decision makers and the perpetuation of colonial power dynamic through technological dependence.

Final Reflection on the implementation of my website

Reflecting on the development of my website over the past few weeks, I feel that I have made significant progress and achieved most of the goals I set out at the beginning of the process. I've established a good base for future websites.

One of the most notable improvements I made was expanding on my portfolio by including and showcasing my 3D models. I felt this was a necessary step to level up my portfolio page. While my game development projects demonstrated many of my skills, they didn't fully represent the range of my abilities. I also recognised that not every user who is viewing my portfolio would want to download and play a game so including interactive 3D models which users can pan, move, rotate and zoom enhances the user experience by making the viewing process more engaging and memorable. Although I have expanded my portfolio page, I do see room to develop it furthering by adding my logo designs and digital art projects

Another key enhancement was implementing a light and dark mode toggle that persists across pages. This enhances both my user interface and overall user experience by offering visual comfort to users and also maintains consistency and personalisation throughout their experience on my website. I also focused on accessibility by adding a modal box for opening and viewing images, allowing users to click on images and view larger versions, improving readability. Additionally, I improved the descriptive quality of alt text and used aria labels for elements with no visible text but needs a description for screen readers to pick up.

My folder structure is consistent across my HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Each of my file and folder names begin with a lowercase letter, to promote clean organisation. I also made sure to use my semantic mark-up correctly, paying special attention to the context and meaning of each tag. Each of my pages are divided into <header>, <main>, <footer> My header contains my <nav> and navigation bar, my main contains the content divided into <sections> and <articles> were appropriate (e.g inside the <main> tag of my blog posts the content is wrapped in an <article>).
